  • Storage: Store in a dry location away from direct sunlight to keep the colors and decals from fading.
  • Material Warnings: Set includes small parts such as play coins and snack pieces. Adult supervision is recommended for younger siblings.

Skills Developed
  • Sorting & Matching: Children learn to match each snack icon to its labeled slot (A1–C3).
  • Fine Motor: Picking up coins, sliding pieces into slots, and pressing buttons strengthens hand coordination and finger dexterity.
  • Cause And Effect: Children learn that pressing a specific numbered button produces a predictable result — the matching snack drops down.
Age Suitability
  • Minimum Safe Age: 4 years
  • Ideal Age Range: 4–8 years
  • Developmental Fit: Children ages 5–7 can usually operate the machine independently, recognize the letter and number codes, and run an extended pretend snack shop on their own.
Play Patterns
  • Primary Play Type: Pretend-play retail and sorting game.
  • Play Structure: Children load snack pieces into labeled slots, then "sell" them by matching a coin and a numbered button to dispense the correct item.
  • Social Context: Works well for solo play or as a two-player "shopkeeper and customer" game with siblings or friends.
  • Adult Involvement: Minimal supervision needed after an initial walkthrough of how the slots and coins work.
  • Play Progression: Kids start by simply sorting snacks into slots, then progress to running a full pretend store with coins and customers.
  • Session Length: Typical play sessions last 15 to 30 minutes per round of "shopping."
